Discover Our Amazing Animals

Explore the diverse wildlife that calls Lanka Pub Zoo home. Learn about their habitats, diets, behaviors, and our conservation efforts.

Sri Lankan Leopard

Panthera pardus kotiya

  • Age: Up to 12-15 years in the wild
  • Diet: Carnivore (Deer, wild boar, monkeys)
  • Behavior: Solitary and nocturnal, excellent climbers. An apex predator.
  • Natural Places: Forests and scrublands across Sri Lanka, particularly dry zone.
  • Conservation Status: Endangered

Asian Elephant

Elephas maximus

  • Age: 40-60 years in the wild
  • Diet: Herbivore (Grasses, leaves, bark, fruits)
  • Behavior: Highly intelligent and social, live in matriarchal herds. Known for their emotional depth.
  • Natural Places: Grasslands and forests throughout Asia.
  • Conservation Status: Endangered

Purple-Faced Langur

Semnopithecus vetulus

  • Age: Up to 20 years
  • Diet: Herbivore/Folivore (Leaves, fruits, flowers)
  • Behavior: Arboreal, social primates. Endemic to Sri Lanka.
  • Natural Places: Wet zone forests of Sri Lanka.
  • Conservation Status: Endangered

Indian Peafowl

Pavo cristatus

  • Age: 10-25 years
  • Diet: Omnivore (Grains, insects, fruits, small reptiles)
  • Behavior: Known for elaborate courtship display of males. Found in open forestlands.
  • Natural Places: Native to the Indian subcontinent, also found in Sri Lanka.
  • Conservation Status: Least Concern

Sri Lankan Sloth Bear

Melursus ursinus inornatus

  • Age: Up to 20 years
  • Diet: Omnivore (Insects, fruits, honey, roots)
  • Behavior: Nocturnal, dig for insects with long claws, known for loud feeding noises.
  • Natural Places: Dry zone forests of Sri Lanka.
  • Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Spotted Deer (Chital)

Axis axis

  • Age: 8-12 years
  • Diet:Herbivore (Grasses, leaves, fruits, agricultural crops)
  • Behavior: Highly gregarious, living in large mixed-sex herds. Active primarily in the morning and late afternoon.
  • Natural Places: Found in forests, grasslands, and open woodlands across the Indian subcontinent, including Sri Lanka.
  • Conservation Status:Least Concern

Lion

Panthera leo

  • Age: 10-14 years in the wild
  • Diet:Carnivore (Large ungulates like wildebeest, zebra, buffalo, antelope)
  • Behavior: Highly social, living in prides. Females do most of the hunting. Roars can be heard up to 8 km away.
  • Natural Places: Savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands of Sub-Saharan Africa and a small population in India (Asiatic Lion).
  • Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Wild Water Buffalo

Bubalus arnee

  • Age: 20-25 years
  • Diet: Herbivore (Mainly grasses and aquatic plants)
  • Behavior: Gregarious, living in herds. Spend much time near water sources. Males often live solitarily or in small bachelor groups.
  • Natural Places: Wetlands, marshes, floodplains, and dense grasslands in parts of India, Nepal, Bhutan, Thailand, and Cambodia.
  • Conservation Status:Endangered

Grey Langur (Hanuman Langur)

Semnopithecus entellus

  • Age: 18-25 years
  • Diet: Herbivore (Leaves, fruits, flowers, buds, bark, occasionally insects)
  • Behavior:Diurnal, highly social, live in troops with complex hierarchies. Excellent climbers and leapers.
  • Natural Places:Widely distributed across the Indian subcontinent, including Sri Lanka's dry zone forests, semi-arid plains, and even urban areas.
  • Conservation Status:Least Concern(though some subspecies may be more threatened)
